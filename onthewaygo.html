On the way go grub

Project Overview

The Product

On-The-Way-Go-Grub App In today's fast-paced world, many people struggle to find time to cook healthy meals. this can lead to unhealthy eating habits and decline in overall well-deing. to address this problem, I designed a cafe app that allows users to order food for take-away, saving them time and effort.

Product Duration

Oct 23 - Dec 23

Problem

In today's fast-paced world, a significant challenge faced by busy professionals is the lack of time for cooking and the inconvenience of of waiting in cafes to order food. balancing hectic schedules with the need for nourishing meals becomes a daily struggle. how minght we develop a solution that caters to the culinary needs of these time-strapped individuals, offering a convenient and efficient alternative to traditional dining options?

Solution

"On-The-Way-Go-Grub": A curated take-away service designed exclusively for busy prefessionals. our solution enables users to order delicious and nutritioufa-spinmeals conveniently from their smartphones,eliminating the need to wait in cafes. with a user-friendly app interface, swift ordering process, and a diverse menu of chef-crafted options, "On-The-Way-Go-Grub" revolutionizes dining for the time-constrained, ensuring a seamless experience for those on the move.

My Role

User Experience Designer, User Interface Designer, Ux Researcher, information Architect, Interaction designer, Visual Designer.

Starting The Design

Paper Wireframe

In the initial stages of our project, I embarked on the process of wireframing to visualize and structure the user interface (UI) design. The wireframes served as foundational blueprints, outlining the layout and functionality of each page within our application. After careful consideration, I selected a wireframe that encapsulated the core elements and navigation flow essential to our project objectives. This wireframe became the cornerstone of our interface design, guiding the development of subsequent pages and functionalities. Utilizing this chosen wireframe as a reference point, I began to iterate and expand upon its framework, gradually fleshing out additional pages and refining the interface elements. This iterative approach allowed for continuous improvement, as each iteration incorporated feedback, user testing insights, and evolving project requirements.

Digital Wireframe & Prototype

Usability Study Findings

Round 1

During the intial stage of the project, I conducted Usability study of my wireframes and prototypes the feedback I received indicates that the user journey was not that intuitive and that users were having difficulty completing tasks. this was a major challenge, as it meant that i needed to redesign the app to make it more user-friendly. , I found that the navigation bar was difficult to find and use, so I redesigned it to be more intuitive. I also reduced the number of columns from three to two, which made the website easier to scan and navigate.

Refinig the design

Mockups

I addressed the Usability issues by iterating on my designs and incorporating feedback from users. I made changes to the layout, navigation, and information architecture of the app to make it easier. After incorporating the feedback received, I proceeded to design the remaining pages. With most of the mockups completed, I'm considering conducting another usability study to gather insights from users regarding the design changes. Their feedback will be invaluable in refining the design further and ensuring it aligns closely with user preferences and needs.

Usability Study Findings

Round 2

With my mockups ready, I decided to conduct another usability test. During the study, I observed that having to choose the delivery option after adding an item to the cart added unnecessary complexity for users. Many participants expressed confusion, stating that they expected to select the delivery type while still on the add-to-cart page. To address this issue, I redesigned the process. Now, on the homepage, users are presented with a pop-up card prompting them to choose the type of delivery before selecting any food items. This adjustment streamlines the user experience by allowing them to make their delivery preference clear upfront, simplifying the subsequent steps of adding items to the cart.
